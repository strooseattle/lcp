Washington State's Roadmap to a Low Carbon Future

The Challenge: How can we have vigorous economic growth and also reduce carbon emissions?

As entrepreneurs and business people deeply committed to the success of Washington State, we are seeking solutions to grow our economy and reduce carbon emissions.

PLAN Washington sets a goal for Washington to be among the top five states in carbon competitiveness (CO2 emitted per million $ of GDP) by the year 2025.

Washington's current ranks: 7th

The Washington State Legislature set a goal to reduce emissions to 1990 levels by 2020, and 25% below 1990 levels by 2035.

The Technology-Enabled Market Strategy

We're leveraging Washington’s immense natural, technological, and human assets to embrace opportunities in the low carbon economy.

In developing the strategy, the Washington Business Alliance identified the major sources of carbon, baseline projections, current policies, and trends. We've targeted technology-enabled pathways to reduce carbon while driving economic prosperity.

Three pillars define our strategy:

#1: Electricity

Build on our state’s natural advantage by leveraging the cleanest and most affordable electricity mix in the nation.

#2: Energy Efficiency

Deploy private sector expertise, tighten building codes, and recover waste energy to ‘free up’ resource capacity for use elsewhere.

#3: Transportation

Stimulate the use of low carbon electricity and fuels in transportation, the largest source of Washington’s emissions.

Sparking Change in Olympia

Electric Vehicle Incentives

Electric and alternative fuel vehicles are an essential strategy for lowering carbon emissions in Washington State. WaBA has advanced this point-of-view through research, advocacy, and formal legislative testimony. Converting gasoline & diesel fleets to electric fuel & natural gas is a great first step. This will require infrastructure build-out. Individual consumers can be encouraged to adopt electric & alternative fuel vehicles through a combonation of incentives & education.

Recently, WaBA played a role in supporting the passage of HB 1853. That bill encourages utility leadership in building out the state's electric vehicle charging infrastructure.

Carbon Reduction Investments

Senate Bill 5735

SB 5735 contains a promising concept to enable utilities to invest in diverse projects that reduce greenhouse gas emissions and receive credit under the state’s Renewable Portfolio Standard, also known as the Energy Independence Act or Initiative 937.

The new approach will empower utilities to explore investments that return the greatest value for both the environment and ratepayers, independent of the technology used. Examples include transportation electrification, converting ferries from diesel to natural gas, or energy storage to increase the capacity of wind and solar for carbon reduction. It may also provide a mechanism for retiring imported coal electricity.
