Washington State's Roadmap to a Low Carbon Future

The Challenge: How can we have vigorous economic growth and also reduce carbon emissions?

As entrepreneurs and business people deeply committed to the success of Washington State, we are seeking solutions to grow our economy and reduce carbon emissions.

PLAN Washington sets a goal for Washington to be among the top five states in carbon competitiveness (CO2 emitted per million $ of GDP) by the year 2025.

Washington's current ranks: 7th

The Washington State Legislature set a goal to reduce emissions to 1990 levels by 2020, and 25% below 1990 levels by 2035.

The Technology-Enabled Market Strategy

We're leveraging Washington’s immense natural, technological, and human assets to embrace opportunities in the low carbon economy.

In developing the strategy, the Washington Business Alliance identified the major sources of carbon, baseline projections, current policies, and trends. We've targeted technology-enabled pathways to reduce carbon while driving economic prosperity.

Three pillars define our strategy:

#1: Electricity

Build on our state’s natural advantage by leveraging the cleanest and most affordable electricity mix in the nation.

#2: Energy Efficiency

Deploy private sector expertise, tighten building codes, and recover waste energy to ‘free up’ resource capacity for use elsewhere.

#3: Transportation

Stimulate the use of low carbon electricity and fuels in transportation, the largest source of Washington’s emissions.
